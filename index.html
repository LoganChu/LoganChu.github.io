About

I'm a junior studying Computer Science at Duke University. I'm driven by the belief that engineering is fundamentally about clear thinking—writing systems that are correct, efficient, and understandable.

My focus areas include systems programming, machine learning fundamentals, and developer tooling. I learn by building: I prefer implementing concepts myself over watching tutorials, and I'm most engaged when working on problems where constraints force interesting tradeoffs.

Outside of coursework, I contribute to open-source projects, experiment with novel architectural patterns, and spend time understanding the theory behind systems I use daily.

Projects

Distributed Key-Value Store

Problem: Understanding how systems maintain consistency across unreliable networks.

Built a fault-tolerant distributed key-value store in Rust supporting RAFT consensus, automatic leader election, and network partitions. The system passes all Maelstrom test suites.

Why interesting: Forced me to reason deeply about ordering guarantees, state machines, and the CAP theorem. Learned that consensus is harder than it appears—most bugs were in edge cases around leadership transitions.

Stack: Rust, Protocol Buffers, async/await

Neural Network from Scratch

Problem: Understanding backpropagation and gradient descent at a fundamental level.

Implemented a fully-connected neural network in NumPy with automatic differentiation. Trained on MNIST, CIFAR-10. Added optimizers (SGD, Adam) and regularization (dropout, batch norm).

Why interesting: Reimplementing PyTorch taught me that neural nets aren't magic— they're carefully orchestrated matrix operations. Understanding the chain rule numerically changed how I think about optimization.

Memory Allocator

Problem: Understanding memory layout and the OS/application boundary.

Implemented a custom allocator in C supporting First-Fit and Best-Fit strategies with coalescing. Achieved fragmentation ratios competitive with jemalloc on synthetic workloads.

Why interesting: Forces you to think about alignment, fragmentation, and syscall overhead. Made real the tradeoff between allocation speed and memory efficiency.
